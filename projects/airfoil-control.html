Active Control of Transitional Airfoil Flows

Overview

This project investigates the use of acoustic forcing to control boundary layer transition and separation on a NACA 65₁-412 airfoil at low Reynolds numbers. The work was conducted at USC's Dryden Wind Tunnel as part of the Summer Provost Fellowship.

Approach

Designed a phase-locked PIV synchronization system coordinating camera, laser, and acoustic speaker. Modernized a 1200+ line MATLAB codebase into modular, multi-run workflows for efficient data processing.

Results

Analyzed lift/drag response and separation behavior under acoustic forcing at various frequencies and amplitudes. Identified optimal forcing parameters for separation control.
