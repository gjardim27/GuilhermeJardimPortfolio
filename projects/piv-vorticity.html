Vorticity Identification from Instantaneous PIV

Overview

Developed a Python-based pipeline to extract vortex core positions and spacing from instantaneous PIV fields in a separated shear layer. The goal is to identify Kelvin-Helmholtz vortex shedding frequencies from spatial data.

Methodology

Computed velocity gradients from PIV snapshots using normalised Gaussian convolution for noise suppression. Applied three vortex identification criteria: spanwise vorticity (ωz), swirling strength (λci), and Q-criterion. Detected discrete vortex cores via thresholding and connected-component labeling on the λci field.

Results

Extracted inter-core spacings across multiple instantaneous frames and converted to temporal frequencies via convection velocity estimates. Generated statistical distributions of vortex spacing and shedding frequency.
